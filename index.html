Asian Owned Businesses You Should Know

As a proud Asian American, I firmly believe it’s always the perfect time to support and celebrate Asian-owned businesses. I’ve compiled a list of some of my favorite AAPI-owned businesses that you can support right now. Some of these Asian-owned brands are also sustainable, with eco-conscious products and practices that are amazing for us and the planet! Click on each business to see pictures of the products they sell and to get more info on each of them.

Omsom

Co-founders and sisters Kim and Vanessa Pham started Omsom to celebrate Asian flavors, stories, and communities. Bringing proud, loud Asian flavors to your fingertips any day of the week, sitting in your pantry right between the tomato sauce and olive oil. No more diluted dishes, no more cultural compromise. Real deal Asian cuisine and communities are too damn delicious to deny.

Tower 28

Tower 28 is a female-owned clean cosmetic brand selling products ranging from lip balms, glosses, highlighters, blushes, bronzers, and more. The Tower 28 team is comprised of longtime beauty industry vets, makeup junkies, and skincare fanatics. Their products are made with non-toxic, non-irritating, good-for-you ingredients that you can pronounce. No fillers. No fragrances. No animal by-products.

Paper and Rice Co

Paper and Rice Co. is a stationery goods and accessories brand focused on creating products with humorous and uplifting themes that feature Asian identity. The purpose of Paper and Rice Co. is to design cards and prints for all occasions and people, Asians being one of them. Our experiences are more universal than you think, so you'll find cards you can relate to too!

Ren

Ren's mission is to keep East Asian traditions alive by shining light on the most coveted stone in the East, jade. Rooted in the sentimental, Ren makes spiritual treasures that connect us to the past, present, and future. We combine natural jadeite with gemstones to explore the interplay between the mystical and physical worlds. Our collection of fine jewelry – celestial and enchanting – is made to last a lifetime and beyond.

Cocokind

You must try cocokind if you love stocking your beauty cabinet with clean and conscious skincare. Their products are nourishing, cruelty-free, and are totally free of artificial fragrances, with great organic options as well. Cocokind is super transparent about their ingredients and impact, so you can make informed decisions when shopping for skincare.

Asian American Girl Club

Asian American Girl Club is an apparel company set to redefine what it means to be a modern Asian American woman. Whether it be through flooding your timeline with powerful images of rad women, creating content -from interviews to skincare to straight up real talk, or designing goodies with you specifically in mind, we’re dedicated to the normalization of the next generation of AAPI gals and boss babes. To give voice and celebrate.

Baggu

Baggu makes reusable bags, masks, and everyday essentials that are adorable and feature the cutest prints. Their designs use sustainable materials, are made to minimize waste, and the brand is committed to ethical and environmentally responsible practices. Baggu even has cases to protect your phone and laptop, and their lightweight totes are built to hold 2-3 times as much as disposable grocery bags.

Chunks

Chunks launched in the Spring of 2019 as a one-woman operation and grew to a team of five women by 2021. Tiffany, the founder of Chunks explained that Chunks designs are inspired by her passion for colors, culture and trends, nostalgic feelings and art like the Memphis design movement. Chunks hair accessories are made from acetate, which is a highly durable, plant-based material with a much shorter life span than typical plastics and acrylics.

Linjer

If you love sleek, minimalist accessories without the luxury markup, you’ll love Linjer, an Asian-owned brand that focuses on sustainability as one of its core values. Co-founded by Jennifer Chong and Roman Khan, the brand selects eco-friendly, long-lasting materials for its unique jewelry, handbags, and watches. I love that they’re committed to quality products and caring for the planet, making them an awesome brand to support this Earth Day.

Blueland

Co-founder and CEO Sarah Paiji Yoo started Blueland after becoming a new mom and realizing the harmful effects of plastic on our environment and water supply. She founded Blueland with the belief that you shouldn’t have to sacrifice a clean home for a clean planet. Now, Blueland makes innovative, eco-friendly products with reusable packaging to help make the Earth a cleaner, better place.

Drifter Organics

This organic, vegan skincare line is every beauty lover’s dream. Drift Organics makes nourishing body butter, body oil, and soap that are gentle, all-natural, non-toxic, and made with care. Their products are clean and super affordable, so you don’t have to break the bank to make your beauty routine more sustainable.

Huppy

Driven to help the environment, David Phan and Cathy Tran co-founded Huppy, a brand whose mission is to make way for more sustainable smiles with their natural, zero-waste, sustainable toothpaste tablets.
